=== "Ingredients"

    - Tangzhong
        - 25 g All-Purpose Flour
        - 125 g Water
    - 170 g Sourdough Starter, ripe
    - 45 g Water, lukewarm
    - 245 g All-Purpose Flour
    - 14 g Nonfat Dry Milk
    - 9 g Salt
    - 13 g Granulated Sugar
    - 57 g Unsalted Butter, softened

    !!! info "Yields 6 buns"

=== "Directions"

    1. **Make tangzhong.** Whisk 25 g flour and 125 g water in a small saucepan
        over medium heat, stirring constantly 2-3 minutes until thickened to a
        paste and temperature reaches 65ºC (149ºF). Cool to room temperature.
    2. **Mix.** Combine starter, water, and tangzhong in the bowl of a stand mixer.
        Add flour, dry milk, salt, and sugar. Mix with the paddle attachment on
        low 2-3 minutes until a sticky, cohesive dough forms.
    3. **Knead.** Switch to the dough hook. Add butter and knead on medium 8-12
        minutes until smooth and elastic. Dough is ready when a small piece
        stretches thin enough to see through (windowpane test).
    4. **Bulk ferment.** Cover and rest in a warm place 75ºC-80ºF (24ºC-27ºC) for 4
        hours. Stretch and fold once per hour for the first 3 hours.
    5. **Divide.** Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and divide into 6
        equal pieces (~120 g each).
    6. **Shape** each piece into a tight ball. Place on a parchment-lined baking
        sheet and press gently to flatten slightly.
    7. **Proof.** Cover and rest in a warm place 4 hours until puffy.
    8. **Preheat oven** to 190ºC (375ºF).
    9. **Egg wash.** Brush buns with beaten egg yolk mixture. Sprinkle with sesame
        seeds if using.
    10. **Bake** 20-25 minutes until golden brown and internal temperature reads
        88ºC (190ºF).
    11. **Cool** at room temperature before serving.

    !!! tip "For overnight proofing..."

        Refrigerate shaped buns after 1-2 hours at room temperature. Remove from the
        refrigerator 1 hour before baking and extend bake time by 2 minutes."

??? question "Why tangzhong?"

    Cooking flour with water before mixing gelatinizes the starch, which locks in
    moisture and produces a softer, more tender crumb that stays fresh longer.
