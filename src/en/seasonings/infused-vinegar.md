=== "Ingredients"

    - 240 g Vinegar
    - Variable amounts of almost anything

    | Infusion                                    | Vinegar     | Best Used For                               |
    | :------------------------------------------ | :---------- | :------------------------------------------ |
    | 10 g Black Peppercorn                       | Apple cider | Dressings, roasted vegetables               |
    | 10 g Dried Chili Flakes                     | Apple cider | Beans, roasted root vegetables, grain bowls |
    | 10 g Garlic Cloves, crushed                 | Red wine    | Marinades, pan sauces, vinaigrettes         |
    | 50 g Fresh Ginger Root, sliced              | White wine  | Fish, chicken, lentil dishes                |
    | 40 g Fresh Ginger Root + 1 strip Lemon Peel | White wine  | Fish, dressings with citrus                 |
    | 3 sprigs Fresh Rosemary                     | Red wine    | Red meat, lamb, bitter greens               |
    | 4 sprigs Fresh Tarragon                     | White wine  | Chicken, eggs, fish                         |

    !!! info "Garlic harmlessly turns blue-green in acid."

        Acidity activates enzymes that react with sulfur-containing amino acids, forming
        polypyrrole pigments. Strain solids and use vinegar normally."

=== "Starting the infusion"

    1. **Sanitize equipment.**
    2. **Combine.** Add ingredients and pour vinegar over at room temperature.
    3. **Steep.** Seal tightly. Steep in cool, dark cupboard.
    4. **Monitor.** Shake gently every few days to redistribute flavors.
    5. **Taste** from day 5. Stop when you're satisfied.
    6. **Strain** all perishable solids from the vinegar.

    ??? warning "Use only glass or stainless steel containers."

        Vinegar reacts with reactive metals and degrades plastic over time.

    !!! info "Stop straining when it tastes right."

        Flavor is the only reliable endpoint; strain when the vinegar tastes right. That
        said:

        - Fresh herbs and sliced roots release their flavour compounds relatively
          quickly and should be tasted from around day 5.
        - Denser, woodier herbs and whole spices extract more slowly and generally need
          closer to two weeks.

    ??? tip "Begin with hot water 70-80ºC (160-180ºF) to reduce steeping time from weeks to days."

        Heat vinegar -- do not boil, as boiling drives off volatile aromatics. Combine.
        Cool before sealing. Steep at room temperature.

=== "Finishing and storing"

    1. **Strain** any solids from the vinegar.
    2. **Store** in cool, dark place.

    !!! info "Store 6-12 months sealed in cool, dark place."
